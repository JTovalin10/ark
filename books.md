# Reading List — Systems Engineering

Ordered by when to read, not importance. Each book is timed to land when you have the coursework to make it click.

---

## Summer at Truveta (June – August 2026)

**3. CS:APP — Computer Systems: A Programmer's Perspective**
*Chapters 6, 8, 9, 10, 11, 12*

You have the OS theory from OSTEP. CS:APP gives you the lower-level hardware perspective that complements it and fills the gaps that show up in kernel interviews — cache hierarchy, signals, virtual memory, Unix I/O, network programming. Read it alongside your kernel patch work.

---

## Fall Senior Year (September – December 2026)

**4. Designing Data-Intensive Applications — Martin Kleppmann**
Read alongside 453. Perfect timing — the course and the book reinforce each other. Go deep on the replication, partitioning, and transactions chapters. This is also the mental model you need for Raft Phase 3.

**5. System Design Interview Vol. 1 — Alex Xu**
One weekend read, just for interview structure. Don't over-invest here — your actual systems knowledge is the substance. Xu gives you the format and the vocabulary interviewers expect.

---

## Winter (January – March 2027)

**6. The Linux Programming Interface — Michael Kerrisk**
*Selected chapters: sockets, processes, threads, epoll*

The practical POSIX reference that goes deeper than UNP on Linux-specific behavior. Read this before interview season. Directly relevant to your kernel patch work and to any systems programming questions at Cloudflare, AWS, or Red Hat.

**7. Site Reliability Engineering — Google**
*Selected chapters*
Free at sre.google/books. Read before GCP applications open. Understand SLOs, error budgets, and the operational philosophy. GCP interviewers will assume you've read it.

---

## Two Weeks Before Interviews

**8. The Datacenter as a Computer — Barroso, Clidaras, Hölzle**
GCP context. Short read. Gives you the mental model for how Google thinks about infrastructure at scale — warehouse-scale computing, tail latency, resource disaggregation. Signals genuine GCP interest in interviews.

**9. TCP/IP Illustrated Vol. 1 — W. Richard Stevens**
*TCP chapters only*
Final deep TCP review for Cloudflare and AWS networking rounds. You know TCP from your networking course — this is the reference-level detail that separates candidates who understand it from candidates who've memorized it.
